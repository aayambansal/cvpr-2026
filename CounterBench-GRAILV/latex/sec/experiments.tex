\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

\subsection{Models Evaluated}

We evaluate six VLMs spanning proprietary and open-weight families:

\noindent\textbf{Proprietary:} GPT-4o~\cite{radford2021clip} (OpenAI, Nov.\ 2024), Claude~3.5 Sonnet (Anthropic), and Gemini~2.0 Flash (Google).

\noindent\textbf{Open-weight:} Qwen2.5-VL 72B (Alibaba), Pixtral Large (Mistral AI), and Llama~3.2 11B Vision (Meta).

All models are accessed via API with temperature $T=0$ for deterministic outputs. We use a standardized system prompt instructing concise answers: ``yes''/``no'' for binary questions, and just the number for counting questions. Answers are normalized by extracting the first yes/no token or numeral.

\paragraph{Key Finding 1: Massive CCS gap despite high accuracy.}
All models achieve $>82\%$ accuracy on original images and $>93\%$ on intervened images, yet their \ccs spans a 19.5-point range (99.7\% to 80.2\%). This demonstrates that standard accuracy substantially overstates counterfactual consistency.

\paragraph{Key Finding 2: Distinct failure profiles.}
Decomposing \ccs reveals two contrasting failure modes. Claude~3.5 Sonnet has the lowest specificity (80.7\%)---it spuriously changes answers for irrelevant interventions. In contrast, Llama~3.2 11B has the lowest sensitivity (77.4\%)---it fails to change answers when the ground truth changes, producing ``sticky'' responses. Pixtral Large shares this sticky-answer pattern (sensitivity 82.5\%). On the other hand, Qwen2.5-VL 72B and Pixtral Large both achieve \emph{perfect specificity} (100\%), never changing answers for irrelevant interventions.

\paragraph{Key Finding 3: Open-weight models can rival proprietary ones.}
Qwen2.5-VL 72B (97.5\% \ccs) outperforms Claude~3.5 Sonnet (87.7\%) and approaches GPT-4o (97.8\%). This demonstrates that model scale and training recipe matter more than proprietary status for counterfactual consistency.

Table~\ref{tab:category_results} reveals a clear difficulty hierarchy:

\paragraph{Easy: Spatial and compositional.} Top models (Gemini, GPT-4o, Qwen2.5-VL, Claude) achieve perfect \ccs. Even the weakest model (Llama~3.2 11B) scores 83--88\%.

\paragraph{Hard: Counting and occlusion.} These expose all models. Counting requires exact numerical tracking under object removal; even GPT-4o drops to 89\%. Occlusion requires understanding visibility constraints---Pixtral drops to 68\% and Llama to 65\%.

\paragraph{Model-dependent: Causal.} Gemini and GPT-4o handle causal reasoning near-perfectly ($\geq$99\%), but Claude drops to 74\%, and open-weight models to 81--83\%, indicating that understanding arrows-as-causation and causal chains remains challenging.
