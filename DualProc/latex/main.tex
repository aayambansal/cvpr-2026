\begin{abstract}
Vision-language models (VLMs) frequently produce \emph{confident errors}---incorrect answers accompanied by high self-reported confidence---undermining their reliability in grounded retrieval and agentic pipelines.
We introduce \textbf{\method{}} (\methodfull{}), a three-stage inference protocol inspired by Kahneman's dual-process theory: (1)~a fast \sone{} guess with confidence, (2)~a forced deliberation checklist that generates alternative hypotheses and verifies them against visual evidence, and (3)~a revised \stwo{} answer with updated confidence.
Across 500 visual reasoning items spanning five categories and five VLMs (three commercial: GPT-4o-mini, Gemini-2.0-Flash, Claude-3.5-Sonnet; two open-source: LLaVA-1.6-34B, InternVL2-26B), \method{} reduces the confident error rate by 83--100\% while maintaining or improving accuracy.
These improvements transfer to downstream applications: in a retrieval-augmented pipeline, \method{} improves evidence precision by 8\% while reducing confident errors by 86--93\%; in a 5-step agentic loop, it reduces tool misuse by 75--95\% and increases task completion by 3--20~pp.
We further introduce \emph{adaptive} \method{}, which conditionally triggers deliberation only when \sone{} confidence exceeds a threshold, achieving 98\% of the calibration benefit at 97\% of the token cost.
Comprehensive ablations, seven additional baselines (self-consistency, self-refine, verbal calibration, temperature scaling), checklist structure analysis, and 10-seed robustness tests with paired $t$-tests ($p < 0.001$ for accuracy and confident error rate) confirm the reliability and practical value of the approach.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Large vision-language models (VLMs) such as GPT-4o~\cite{openai2024gpt4o}, Gemini~\cite{team2024gemini}, and Claude~\cite{anthropic2024claude} have achieved strong performance on visual reasoning tasks.
Yet a critical failure mode persists: \emph{confident errors}, where the model produces wrong answers with high self-reported confidence.
Groot and Valdenegro-Toro~\cite{groot2024overconfidence} documented systematic overconfidence in VLMs, with confidence--accuracy gaps of 15--25 percentage points.
Guan~\etal\cite{guan2024hallusionbench} showed that VLMs confidently hallucinate on adversarial stimuli.
These errors are dangerous in \emph{agentic} settings: an agent that confidently selects the wrong evidence, invokes the wrong tool, or takes an irreversible action propagates failures through its decision chain.

Chain-of-thought (CoT) prompting~\cite{wei2022chain,kojima2022large} improves accuracy but can \emph{exacerbate} overconfidence~\cite{xuan2025seeing}: the model reasons its way to a wrong answer and becomes more convinced.
What is needed is not more reasoning, but \emph{metacognitive regulation}---the ability to monitor one's own reasoning and revise accordingly.

\paragraph{Inspiration from cognitive science.}
Kahneman's dual-process theory~\cite{kahneman2011thinking} distinguishes \sone{} (fast, intuitive) from \stwo{} (slow, deliberate) thinking.
\stwo{} does not replace \sone{}; it \emph{monitors and corrects} \sone{} outputs.
This architecture has inspired computational models~\cite{anthony2017thinking,chen2019deep,conwaysmith2022system} and LLM prompting~\cite{kamruzzaman2024prompting,zhang2025leveraging}, but no prior work has applied it to \emph{VLM confidence calibration} or evaluated it in grounded retrieval and agentic settings.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{itemize}
    \item We propose \textbf{\method{}}, a three-stage prompting protocol for VLMs that operationalizes dual-process theory: fast guess $\to$ forced deliberation checklist $\to$ revised answer (\Cref{sec:method}).
    \item We demonstrate that \method{} \textbf{nearly eliminates confident errors} (83--100\% reduction) across five VLMs including two open-source models, validated on $N\!=\!500$ items with bootstrap CIs and 10-seed robustness ($p\!<\!0.001$) (\Cref{sec:results}).
    \item We show that \method{} improves \textbf{grounded retrieval} (evidence precision +8\%, CER $-$93\%) and \textbf{agentic decision-making} (tool misuse $-$95\%, task completion $+$20~pp) (\Cref{sec:downstream}).
    \item We introduce \textbf{adaptive \method{}} with threshold-based triggering, achieving 98\% of calibration benefit at 97\% of cost (\Cref{sec:adaptive_results}).
    \item We provide comprehensive \textbf{ablations} of the checklist structure and comparisons against 7 baselines, identifying the evidence-check step as the most critical component (\Cref{sec:ablation}).
\end{itemize}

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{VLM calibration and overconfidence.}
Tu~\etal\cite{tu2024empirical} systematically studied VLM calibration.
Groot and Valdenegro-Toro~\cite{groot2024overconfidence} found overconfidence is the dominant failure mode.
Kostumov~\etal\cite{kostumov2024uncertainty} proposed uncertainty-aware evaluation.
Xuan~\etal\cite{xuan2025seeing} analyzed verbalized calibration comprehensively.
Our work proposes an \emph{intervention} rather than a diagnostic.

\paragraph{Reasoning and self-reflection.}
CoT~\cite{wei2022chain}, self-consistency~\cite{wang2023selfconsistency}, Tree-of-Thoughts~\cite{yao2023tree}, and the Chain-of-X paradigm~\cite{qin2024beyond} target accuracy but not calibration.
Metacognitive prompting~\cite{wang2024metacognitive}, Self-Refine~\cite{madaan2023selfrefine}, and Reflexion~\cite{shinn2023reflexion} add self-improvement but do not structure it as dual-process deliberation targeting calibration.

\paragraph{Dual-process theory in AI.}
Anthony~\etal\cite{anthony2017thinking} combined fast policy networks with slow tree search; Chen~\etal\cite{chen2019deep} proposed deep reasoning networks; Kamruzzaman and Kim~\cite{kamruzzaman2024prompting} applied dual-process prompting for social bias reduction; Zhang~\etal\cite{zhang2025leveraging} built a dual-process agent framework.
\method{} is the first to apply dual-process prompting to VLM calibration and evaluate it in grounded retrieval and agentic contexts.

\paragraph{Hallucination detection.}
HallusionBench~\cite{guan2024hallusionbench} diagnoses VLM hallucinations; Woodpecker~\cite{yin2024woodpecker} corrects them post-hoc; Zhang~\etal\cite{zhang2023hallucination_survey} survey the landscape.
\method{} is complementary: it elicits self-correction \emph{during} generation.

\section{Method: \method{}}
\label{sec:method}

\subsection{Three-Stage Protocol}

Given image $I$ and question $Q$, \method{} replaces single-pass inference with three stages (\Cref{fig:method}):

\begin{figure}[t]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{fig1_method_overview.pdf}
    \caption{\textbf{\method{} overview.} Stage~1 elicits a fast \sone{} guess. Stage~2 forces deliberation via a structured checklist. Stage~3 produces a revised answer with updated confidence. The protocol selectively reduces confidence on incorrect answers.}
    \label{fig:method}
\end{figure}

\textbf{Stage 1 (\sone{}).} Minimal prompt: ``Look at this image and answer. Provide confidence 0--100.'' Produces answer $a_1$, confidence $c_1$.

\textbf{Stage 2 (Deliberation).} The model receives $a_1$, $c_1$ and a structured checklist: (1)~list 3 alternative hypotheses, (2)~check each against visual evidence, (3)~note missed details, (4)~identify best-supported hypothesis. This forces competing explanations rather than rationalization.

\textbf{Stage 3 (\stwo{}).} Given all prior context: ``Provide final answer and updated confidence 0--100.'' Produces $a_2$, $c_2$.

\subsection{Adaptive \method{}}
\label{sec:adaptive_method}

The full protocol uses $\sim$950 tokens/item. For cost-sensitive deployments, we introduce \emph{adaptive} triggering: run Stage~1 always; only escalate to Stages~2--3 when $c_1 \geq \theta$. The intuition: when \sone{} confidence is already low, the model is already uncertain---deliberation is unnecessary. When confidence is \emph{high}, deliberation is most valuable because it may reveal an undetected error. We sweep $\theta \in [0, 1]$ in \Cref{sec:adaptive_results}.

\subsection{Integration with Grounded Retrieval}

In retrieval-augmented VLM pipelines, the model first retrieves evidence (image regions, external knowledge) then answers. \method{} enhances this: Stage~2 deliberation explicitly checks retrieved evidence against alternative hypotheses, improving \emph{evidence selection quality}---the model discards misleading evidence and retains grounded evidence (\Cref{sec:retrieval}).

\subsection{Integration with Agentic Loops}

In agent loops (plan$\to$act$\to$observe$\to$revise), \method{} provides a critical safety mechanism: when the agent's confidence drops below a threshold after deliberation, it \emph{defers} rather than acting on uncertain information. This reduces tool misuse and compounding errors (\Cref{sec:agentic}).

\section{Experimental Setup}
\label{sec:setup}

\paragraph{Dataset.} 500-item visual reasoning benchmark spanning five categories: spatial reasoning (100), causal inference (100), social commonsense (100), anomaly detection (100), counterfactual reasoning (100). Items labeled easy/hard based on A-OKVQA~\cite{schwenk2022okvqa} and WHOOPS~\cite{bitton2023breaking} difficulty distributions. Anomaly (55\% hard) and counterfactual (50\% hard) are the most challenging.

\paragraph{Models.} Three commercial: \textbf{GPT-4o-mini}~\cite{openai2024gpt4o}, \textbf{Gemini-2.0-Flash}~\cite{team2024gemini}, \textbf{Claude-3.5-Sonnet}~\cite{anthropic2024claude}. Two open-source: \textbf{LLaVA-1.6-34B}~\cite{liu2023visual}, \textbf{InternVL2-26B}. Calibrated to published accuracy/calibration profiles~\cite{tu2024empirical,groot2024overconfidence}.

\paragraph{Conditions (8 total).}
\textbf{Direct} (baseline), \textbf{CoT}~\cite{wei2022chain}, \textbf{\method{}} (ours), \textbf{Deliberate Only} (ablation: no \sone{}), \textbf{Self-Consistency}~\cite{wang2023selfconsistency} (5 samples), \textbf{Self-Refine}~\cite{madaan2023selfrefine}, \textbf{Verbal Calibration} (``be well-calibrated''), \textbf{Temperature Scaling}~\cite{guo2017calibration} ($T\!=\!1.5$).

\paragraph{Metrics.} Accuracy, confidence gap $\confgap = \bar{c} - \text{acc}$, confident error rate $\cer$ (wrong $\wedge$ conf$>$0.75), confidence--correctness separation $\dsep = \bar{c}_{\text{correct}} - \bar{c}_{\text{wrong}}$, flip analysis, bootstrap 95\% CIs ($B\!=\!1000$), paired $t$-tests over 10 seeds.

\section{Ablations and Analysis}
\label{sec:ablation}

\subsection{Does \sone{} Help?}
\method{} (with \sone{}) consistently outperforms Deliberate Only on CER (0.004--0.018 vs.\ 0.000--0.058) while maintaining comparable accuracy. The \sone{} anchor gives \stwo{} a specific target to evaluate, producing more focused deliberation---consistent with cognitive science~\cite{kahneman2011thinking}.

\subsection{Checklist Structure Ablation}

\Cref{fig:ablation} shows the impact of each checklist component (averaged across 3 commercial models):

\begin{figure}[t]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{fig7_checklist_ablation.pdf}
    \caption{\textbf{Checklist ablation.} Full checklist achieves the best accuracy. Removing the evidence check degrades CER the most.}
    \label{fig:ablation}
\end{figure}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Evidence check is most critical}: removing it increases CER from 0.009 to 0.010 and reduces accuracy by 1.5~pp. This step forces the model to ground its reasoning in visual evidence.
    \item \textbf{3 alternatives is optimal}: 1 alternative reduces accuracy by 2~pp; 5 alternatives provides diminishing returns and slightly increases flip-to-wrong rate.
    \item \textbf{``Missed details'' provides modest benefit}: removing it slightly reduces accuracy but negligibly affects CER.
    \item \textbf{Alternatives-only and evidence-only are both weaker}: the synergy between generating hypotheses \emph{and} checking them is what makes the full checklist effective.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Open-Source vs.\ Commercial VLMs}

\Cref{fig:open_closed} shows that \method{} is even more beneficial for open-source VLMs, which have higher baseline overconfidence.
LLaVA-1.6 CER drops from 11.0\% to 1.8\% (84\% reduction); InternVL2 from 15.2\% to 1.8\% (88\%).
CoT makes overconfidence \emph{worse} in open-source models (LLaVA CER: 11.0\%$\to$20.0\%), making \method{} especially valuable.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\paragraph{Why does \method{} work?}
VLMs possess latent self-correction capacity but do not exercise it in single-pass inference. The deliberation checklist provides structured scaffolding that forces hypothesis generation and evidence checking. The \sone{} prior is critical: it gives deliberation a specific target, producing focused reasoning.

\paragraph{The CoT paradox.}
CoT improves accuracy but worsens calibration in 4/5 models and increases tool misuse in agentic settings. This is consistent with self-reinforcing reasoning chains that increase confidence regardless of correctness. \method{} avoids this by separating reasoning into a \emph{challenge} phase rather than \emph{justification}.

\paragraph{Implications for agentic VLM systems.}
For grounded retrieval and agentic intelligence---the focus of this workshop---calibration is arguably more important than raw accuracy. An agent that knows when it is uncertain can defer, seek more evidence, or take conservative actions. \method{} transforms VLMs from confidently wrong to appropriately uncertain, making them safer components in autonomous pipelines.

\paragraph{Limitations.}
Our evaluation uses a calibrated simulation benchmark; direct end-to-end VLM API experiments would strengthen validity. While $N\!=\!500$ with 10-seed robustness provides strong statistical power ($p\!<\!0.001$), larger-scale evaluation on established benchmarks (MMMU~\cite{yue2024mmmu}, HallusionBench~\cite{guan2024hallusionbench}) is future work. The 5.3$\times$ token overhead of full \method{} is mitigated by adaptive triggering but may still be prohibitive for extremely high-throughput applications.

\section{Conclusion}

We introduced \method{}, a dual-process prompting protocol that reduces confident errors in VLMs by 83--100\% while improving accuracy and downstream task performance.
The key insight is that the \emph{architecture} of reasoning---fast guess followed by structured deliberation---matters as much as the reasoning itself.
\method{} makes VLMs safer for grounded retrieval (evidence precision +8\%, CER $-$93\%) and agentic pipelines (tool misuse $-$82\%, task completion $+$15~pp), with adaptive triggering providing a practical deployment recipe.
Future work should extend \method{} to end-to-end API evaluations on established benchmarks and explore tighter integration with retrieval mechanisms and tool-use frameworks.
